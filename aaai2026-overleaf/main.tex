\begin{abstract}
Many prediction systems output independent scores for labels or concepts that are constrained by a large symbolic ontology. The statistically correct structure-aware posterior is obtained by conditioning the product distribution on the ontology theory, giving marginals that are ratios of weighted model counts (WMC). This target is exact but intractable on large ontologies; the scalable alternative used in practice is a soft message-passing closure whose correctness has not been characterized. We study this gap for true-path ontology inference, a setting that covers ontology-constrained prediction and neuro-symbolic concept layers. We prove that the soft closure is loopy belief propagation on the true-path constraint graph: it is exact on tree-like fragments for all priors, and its first failure mode is path reconvergence. We then introduce separator-aware modular WMC, which cuts a small reconvergence core, solves low-treewidth peripheral modules exactly, and represents the remaining core dependence by a rank-$r$ separator message. On the Gene Ontology (GO), this recovers exact WMC marginals for 91--100\% of terms across cellular component, molecular function, and biological process; the rank-$1$ endpoint is the common independence closure, while full rank recovers exact core inference.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Many AI systems predict over concept vocabularies whose labels are not independent. Protein function predictors output scores over the Gene Ontology (GO) \citep{ashburner2000go,geneontology2023,kulmanov2020deepgoplus,kulmanov2022deepgozero,yu2023clean}; biomedical annotation systems use anatomy and chemical ontologies such as UBERON and ChEBI \citep{mungall2012uberon,hastings2016chebi}; neuro-symbolic (NeSy) systems attach neural concept scores to symbolic theories \citep{manhaeve2018deepproblog,xu2018semanticloss,ahmed2022spl}. In all of these settings, the predictor emits one marginal score $p_i$ per concept while the ontology imposes implications: if a specific concept holds, its more general ancestors must also hold. The inference problem is to turn the independent product $q(\bC y)=\prod_i p_i^{y_i}(1-p_i)^{1-y_i}$ into probabilities that respect the ontology.

The correct target is clear. Given an ontology theory $T$, one should condition $q$ on the satisfying assignments of $T$ and report the structure-aware marginal
\[
\mu_i = p_T(y_i{=}1)=\frac{\WMC(T\wedge y_i;\bC p)}{\WMC(T;\bC p)}.
\]
These WMC marginals are the natural target for constrained prediction, and coincide with the semantic-probabilistic-layer target in NeSy systems \citep{xu2018semanticloss,ahmed2022spl}. The difficulty is computational: exact WMC is $\#$P-hard and, after compilation, scales exponentially in the treewidth of the ontology constraint graph \citep{chavira2008wmc,darwiche2011sdd,bodlaender1993treewidth}. Thus exact compilers are reliable on small subgraphs but do not reach the largest GO namespaces. Practical systems therefore use fast soft closures or truncated proof provenance, gaining scale while losing a theorem that says when the returned marginals are correct.

This paper studies the true-path theory of hierarchical biomedical ontologies: the propositional grounding of subsumption edges, together with the Horn $\mathcal{EL}^{++}$ normal-form definitions used by OWL~2~EL reasoners \citep{baader2005elenvelope,kazakov2014elk}. In this setting, the soft closure has a simple interpretation: it is loopy belief propagation on the true-path constraint graph. That observation turns a heuristic post-processing step into an object that can be analyzed. It is exact on tree-like fragments, and its error is caused by reconvergent paths that reuse the same evidence.

The same observation also suggests a scalable exact route. Large ontologies are not uniformly hard: high treewidth is concentrated around reconvergent regions, while much of the graph remains low-treewidth once those regions are separated. We therefore use exact WMC where the graph permits it and isolate the remaining dependence at module boundaries. A separator message over the boundary variables carries that dependence; a rank-$1$ separator recovers the independence approximation, while higher rank carries progressively richer boundary joints.

Our contributions are:
\begin{enumerate}
\item We characterize the common soft true-path closure as loopy belief propagation. We prove exactness on tree fragments for all priors, identify reconvergence as the first failure mode, and formalize the core finite and parametric claims in Lean~4.
\item We introduce separator-aware modular WMC. The method cuts a reconvergence core, solves each low-treewidth augmented module exactly, and represents the residual boundary dependence with a rank-$r$ separator.
\item We evaluate the resulting inference layer on GO at ontology scale, include completed UBERON and ChEBI transfer runs, and compare with exact compilation, soft closure, top-$k$ provenance, and learned neuro-symbolic inference where each route is applicable.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Related Work}\label{sec:related}
\paragraph{WMC and knowledge compilation.}
Weighted model counting reduces probabilistic inference to weighted evaluation of a propositional theory \citep{chavira2008wmc}. Knowledge-compilation targets such as SDDs make this exact once the theory is compiled \citep{darwiche2002kcmap,darwiche2011sdd}. The bottleneck is structural: exact compilation and junction-tree inference scale exponentially in treewidth \citep{bodlaender1993treewidth}, which is why monolithic exact WMC walls below large GO namespaces in our experiments.

\paragraph{Ontology reasoning and constrained prediction.}
OWL~2~EL reasoners such as ELK provide scalable logical closure for large biomedical ontologies \citep{baader2005elenvelope,kazakov2014elk}. Protein function and biomedical annotation models then produce per-term scores over these ontologies \citep{kulmanov2018deepgo,kulmanov2020deepgoplus,kulmanov2022deepgozero,yu2023clean}. These systems need posterior marginals that are both structure-aware and calibrated to the independent scores. Our work is not a new neural predictor or ontology reasoner; it is the probabilistic correction layer between independent scores and ontology-constrained marginals.

\paragraph{Learning and inference with label constraints.}
Hierarchical and constrained prediction has a long history in structured and multi-label learning \citep{silla2011survey}. A common route is to train with constraints, for example through posterior regularization or semantic regularization \citep{ganchev2010posterior,diligenti2017semantic}; another is constrained inference at prediction time. Recent analyses distinguish these two roles and show that constrained inference can correct violations even when regularization changes the learned model bias \citep{wang2023regularization}. Our setting follows the inference-side view, but targets calibrated posterior marginals under a large ontology theory rather than only point predictions or violation penalties.

\paragraph{Neuro-symbolic inference layers.}
ProbLog and DeepProbLog compile a logic program to a circuit and evaluate WMC exactly \citep{deraedt2007problog,manhaeve2018deepproblog}. Semantic loss and the semantic probabilistic layer (SPL) use the same WMC marginals $\mu_i$ as training signal \citep{xu2018semanticloss,ahmed2022spl}. Scallop keeps only the top-$k$ proofs per fact \citep{huang2021scallop,li2023scallop}, scaling to full GO but undercounting at reconvergent nodes when $k$ is small. A-NeSI and NeSyDM learn to approximate WMC and avoid Monte-Carlo collapse on synthetic chains \citep{vankrieken2023anesi,vankrieken2025nesydm}, but require per-task training. These systems are important comparison points, but the inference target studied here is broader than any particular NeSy language.

\section{Problem Setup}\label{sec:problem}
\paragraph{Inputs and target.}
A vocabulary $\mathcal{Y}=\{1,\dots,m\}$ lists ontology concepts, one Boolean variable $y_i$ per concept. A predictor emits independent marginals $p_i\in[0,1]$, inducing
\[
q(\bC y)=\prod_{i=1}^{m}p_i^{y_i}(1-p_i)^{1-y_i}.
\]
A symbolic theory $T$ encodes ontology constraints over $\mathcal{Y}$. The target of inference is not the independent $p_i$, but the structure-conditioned marginal $\mu_i=p_T(y_i{=}1)$ obtained after impossible assignments are removed and the remaining mass is renormalized.

\paragraph{Inference routes.}
We compare four inference routes that share the same input---independent marginals $p_i$ and theory $T$---but differ in how they approximate the target $\mu_i$. \textit{Exact WMC} conditions $q$ on $T$ by weighted model counting. \textit{Soft closure} iterates monotone soft-OR updates (the ELK completion \citep{kazakov2014elk}), the common fast post-processing route. \textit{Top-$k$ provenance} keeps only a bounded number of proofs. Our \textit{modular exact WMC} hybridizes exact inference and soft boundary messages: exact junction-tree inference on peripheral modules and a dialable separator on a small reconvergence core (\S\ref{sec:scaling}).

\section{Method: True-Path Inference Layer}\label{sec:setup}

\subsection{Preliminaries}\label{sec:prelim}
We use four objects throughout the paper: the ontology theory, the exact WMC posterior, the soft closure, and the graph structure that controls tractability.

\begin{definition}[True-path theory]\label{def:truepath}
A theory $T$ is a propositional formula over $\mathcal Y$. The \emph{true-path} theory is
\begin{equation*}
\begin{aligned}
T={}&\bigwedge_{(c,\pi)\in E}\bigl(y_c\!\Rightarrow\!y_\pi\bigr)\;\wedge\\
&\bigwedge_{A\sqcap B\sqsubseteq E}\bigl(y_A\wedge y_B\!\Rightarrow\!y_E\bigr)\;\wedge\;(\text{exist.\ defs}),
\end{aligned}
\end{equation*}
the hierarchy edges $E$ plus the EL\textsuperscript{++} normal-form definitions
\citep{baader2005elenvelope,kazakov2014elk}.
\end{definition}

The first conjunction is the true-path rule: when a term $c$ holds, every more general parent $\pi$ holds. The remaining conjuncts keep the Horn definitions produced by $\mathcal{EL}^{++}$ normalization. Thus $T$ is not merely a tree of edges; it is the Boolean grounding of the Horn fragment used by OWL~2~EL biomedical ontologies.

\begin{definition}[WMC marginal]\label{def:wmc}
The structure-conditioned posterior is $p_T(\bC y)\propto[\bC y\models T]\,q(\bC y)$, with target
marginal
\begin{equation*}
\mu_i=p_T(y_i{=}1)=\frac{\WMC(T\wedge y_i;\bC p)}{\WMC(T;\bC p)},
\end{equation*}
\begin{equation*}
\WMC(\phi;\bC p)=\sum_{\bC y\models\phi}\prod_i p_i^{y_i}(1-p_i)^{1-y_i}.
\end{equation*}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Soft true-path update]\label{def:soft}
For a parent concept $\pi$ with prior $q_\pi$ and incoming child messages $m_1,\dots,m_k$, the tree-local soft update is
\[
S_\pi(m_1,\dots,m_k)=
\frac{q_\pi}{q_\pi+(1-q_\pi)\prod_{j=1}^{k}(1-m_j)}.
\]
The \emph{soft closure} iterates these local updates, together with the true-path cap, to a monotone fixpoint.
\end{definition}

On a tree this update is exact: the child subproblems are independent after conditioning on the parent. On a reconvergence, as in the diamond, two incoming messages may share evidence; treating them as independent is the approximation. In the theoretical section we show that this is precisely loopy belief propagation (BP) \citep{pearl1988probabilistic} on the true-path constraint graph.

\begin{definition}[Constraint graph and treewidth]\label{def:graph}
The constraint graph $G_T$ has one node per ontology variable and connects variables that occur together in a clause of $T$. Its treewidth $\tw(G_T)$ is the minimum, over tree decompositions, of the largest bag size minus one.
\end{definition}

Treewidth measures the size of the largest group of variables that exact inference must reason about jointly. Knowledge compilation and junction-tree WMC have cost exponential in $\tw(G_T)$ \citep{darwiche2011sdd,bodlaender1993treewidth}. This is why a forest is easy, the diamond is the first non-tree obstruction, and GO biological process with $\tw=269$ is out of reach for monolithic exact WMC.

\paragraph{Layer overview.}
Figure~\ref{fig:pipeline} summarizes the route used in the rest of the paper. Independent predictor scores enter the exact target of Definition~\ref{def:wmc}; the soft closure approximates it by Definition~\ref{def:soft}; our modular layer restores exact inference on most variables by cutting out the high-treewidth core.

\section{Theoretical Analysis}\label{sec:lean}

The soft closure is the scalable approximation used in place of the \#P-hard exact WMC \citep{chavira2008wmc}, so the central question is \emph{when the cheap closure coincides with it, and where it cannot}. A deployable layer needs this as a theorem. We prove it and formalise the development in Lean~4 \citep{demoura2021lean}. The implementation and axiom footprint are deferred to Appendix~\ref{app:lean}.

The formalisation has two parts. A parametric \texttt{mathlib} development proves
Theorem~\ref{thm:star} in full, over all $k:\mathbb{N}$ and all real priors
(\texttt{WmcStar.star\_marginal\_eq\_softOR}, axiom footprint
$\{\texttt{propext},\texttt{Classical.choice},\texttt{Quot.sound}\}$, no \texttt{sorry}). A
finite Lean-core development kernel-checks the concrete instances and the reconvergence
counterexample by \texttt{decide}, with no \texttt{sorry}, no \texttt{native\_decide}, axiom
footprint $\{\texttt{propext}\}$ alone. Both are in Appendix~\ref{app:lean}.

\paragraph{Tree-exactness for all priors.}
The inductive primitive is the \emph{star}: a target $\pi$ with $k$ children $c_1,\dots,c_k$ under the constraints $y_{c_j}\!\Rightarrow\!y_\pi$. This is the elementary tree fragment, where child subproblems share no evidence except through $\pi$.

\begin{theorem}[Tree-exactness, all priors]\label{thm:star}
For a target $\pi$ with $k$ independent children $c_1,\dots,c_k$ under the true-path constraint $y_{c_j}\!\Rightarrow\!y_\pi$, the exact WMC marginal of $\pi$ equals the soft-OR update
\[
\mu_\pi=\frac{q_\pi}{q_\pi+(1-q_\pi)\prod_{j=1}^{k}(1-q_{c_j})}
\]
for all $k\in\mathbb{N}$ and all priors $q_\pi,q_{c_1},\dots,q_{c_k}$, where $q_i$ denotes the local prior of concept $i$.
\end{theorem}

The proof telescopes the product-of-sums in the numerator over all $2^k$ child assignments to $q_\pi$ (each child factor sums to $q_i+(1-q_i)=1$) and adds the single parent-false world contributing $(1-q_\pi)\prod_i(1-q_i)$, so the marginal is the soft-OR ratio for every $k$ and prior. The Lean development carries out exactly this argument over \texttt{Fin}\,$k$ children and real priors (\texttt{WmcStar.star\_marginal\_eq\_softOR}, via the product-of-sums interchange \texttt{Finset.prod\_univ\_sum}), and the Lean-core battery additionally reduces both sides to the same rational by \texttt{decide} for the single edge and the $k{=}2,3$ stars. Theorem~\ref{thm:star} is therefore the instance, for the true-path theory, of the classical fact that BP is exact on trees \citep{pearl1988probabilistic}: combining messages as independent is justified precisely when the children are independent.

\paragraph{The error is confined to reconvergences.}
\emph{Where} does the closure deviate from WMC? BP on a loopy graph is exact on the computation-tree unrolling: every repeated visit to a variable is copied into a new node. Therefore the update is exact when this unrolling changes nothing, i.e., when the constraint graph is a \emph{polytree} (there is at most one undirected path between any two variables). The obstruction is a \emph{reconvergence}: two derivation paths meet again at a shared variable. On the diamond ($d\!\Rightarrow\!b,c$, $b,c\!\Rightarrow\!a$), exact WMC gives $\mu_a=5/6$ (Lean: \texttt{diamond\_exact\_5\_6}) while the path-independent soft-OR gives $9/10$ (\texttt{diamond\_softor\_9\_10}); the two differ exactly because both messages into $a$ reuse $d$.

\paragraph{Unconditional convergence.}
The synchronous true-path step is extensive and monotone, so in a finite vocabulary it reaches the least fixpoint within $\mathrm{depth}$ steps. Convergence is unconditional, only the limit is structure-dependent. A closed-form error bound on general DAGs is \#P-hard and is reported empirically in \S\ref{sec:experiments}.

\section{Modular Exact WMC}\label{sec:scaling}
Theorem~\ref{thm:star} establishes \emph{when} the soft closure is correct. A deployable layer must also answer \emph{where} exact WMC can still be recovered on a large ontology. We use the diamond lesson at scale: keep tree-like regions exact, isolate reconvergent structure, and represent only the boundary dependence approximately when necessary.

\paragraph{Exact WMC is governed by treewidth.}
Exact $\mu_i$ proceeds by compiling $T$ to an SDD, equivalently by junction-tree calibration, at cost $\Theta(m\,2^{\tw})$ in the constraint-graph treewidth $\tw$ \citep{darwiche2011sdd,darwiche2002kcmap,bodlaender1993treewidth}, $m=|\mathcal Y|$. On a forest $\tw=1$ and exact WMC coincides with the single-pass BP of Theorem~\ref{thm:star}. Each reconvergence enlarges the largest junction-tree bag.

\begin{definition}[Core, module, and boundary]\label{def:module}
Let $C\subseteq\mathcal{Y}$ be a set of variables removed from $G_T$. The connected components of $G_T\setminus C$ are \emph{peripheral modules} $M_1,\dots,M_s$. The boundary of a module is $B_j=\{c\in C: c \text{ is adjacent to some } m\in M_j\}$. We solve the \emph{augmented module} $M_j\cup B_j$ exactly.
\end{definition}

In the diamond example, choosing $C=\{a\}$ leaves the low-treewidth periphery $\{b,c,d\}$ and boundary $B=\{a\}$. In GO, the same idea is applied to high-degree reconvergence hubs. The residual graph alone is not enough: exactness of a module is governed by the treewidth of the augmented module $M_j\cup B_j$, because boundary variables still couple the module to the core.

\begin{definition}[Separator message and rank]\label{def:separator}
A separator message for module $M_j$ is a distribution over its boundary variables $B_j$ supplied by the core. Rank~1 means this boundary distribution is represented as a product of one-variable marginals. A rank-$r$ separator uses a tensor-train factorization of the boundary joint with internal rank at most $r$; full rank represents the exact boundary joint.
\end{definition}

Rank~1 is exactly the independence approximation made by the soft closure in the diamond: the two messages into $a$ are treated as if they came from independent evidence. Increasing the rank allows the separator to carry shared evidence across the boundary.

\paragraph{Separator-aware modular exact WMC.}
We select a boundary-aware core until every augmented module has $\tw\le14$, then run exact junction-tree WMC on each $M_j\cup B_j$. The core sends a separator message to each module; with a full-rank separator, the global result matches exact WMC on the covered variables, while rank~1 recovers the product soft-boundary approximation.

\begin{proposition}[Modular exact WMC]\label{prop:modular}
Given a core $C$ and modules $M_j$ with boundaries $B_j$, suppose each augmented module $M_j\cup B_j$ has bounded treewidth and is solved exactly by junction-tree WMC. If the separator message over each $B_j$ equals the exact core-induced boundary joint, then the resulting marginals on $\bigcup_j M_j$ equal the exact WMC marginals. If the separator is approximated, the only error source is that boundary approximation.
\end{proposition}

\paragraph{Rank-$r$ separator dial.}
At the core boundary the rank-1 product message is the soft-closure endpoint; a rank-$r$ tensor-train separator moves toward the exact boundary joint without changing the exact per-module circuits. The experiments measure this dial on GO cores (Appendix~\ref{app:nesy}, Table~\ref{tab:rank}).

\section{Experiments}\label{sec:experiments}

\paragraph{Experimental design.}
We separate two questions that are often conflated. The first is an \emph{intrinsic inference-layer evaluation}: given independent term scores and an ontology theory, can the layer compute the WMC posterior, where does the soft approximation fail, and what is the cost of the modular construction? This is not a downstream prediction benchmark; it tests the correctness, scalability, and error localization of the inference operator itself. The second is a \emph{downstream post-processing evaluation}: on a standard GO protein-function prediction task, with the backbone fixed, does replacing raw or heuristic ontology correction with modular WMC improve constrained predictions?

\subsection{Intrinsic Inference-Layer Evaluation}\label{sec:intrinsic}
\paragraph{Ontologies and scores.}
We evaluate the true-path inference layer on GO \citep{ashburner2000go,geneontology2023} at namespace scale---cellular component (cc), molecular function (mf), and biological process (bp)---and use UBERON and ChEBI \citep{mungall2012uberon,hastings2016chebi} as cross-ontology transfer cases. Each ontology is instantiated as the same Horn true-path theory, so differences in Table~\ref{tab:ontology-struct} reflect ontology structure rather than task-specific preprocessing.

All intrinsic methods receive the same independent marginals $p_i$ and the same ontology theory $T$. Unless otherwise stated, intrinsic priors are sampled uniformly in $[0.05,0.95]$ under a fixed seed. These priors are not meant to model a protein predictor; they provide controlled inputs for comparing inference operators against the same WMC target.

\paragraph{Intrinsic targets, metrics, and baselines.}
The gold target is the WMC marginal $\mu_i$ in Definition~\ref{def:wmc}. We report monolithic treewidth, residual treewidth after removing reconvergence cores, number of peripheral modules, maximum augmented-module treewidth, exact-coverage rate, wall time, and drift from exact WMC when exact comparison is available. Junction-tree WMC and brute force agree to machine precision on every enumerated validation module. Baselines include soft closure, monolithic exact WMC/DeepProbLog-style compilation, Scallop top-$k$ provenance, and learned neuro-symbolic inference systems on the tasks they can instantiate. A-NeSI and NeSyDM are not direct ontology-scale baselines because they require per-task training; A-NeSI also requires exact-WMC samples, which are precisely unavailable on full GO bp. We therefore report them only on the carry-chain benchmark in Appendix~\ref{app:nesy}.

\subsubsection{Reconvergence Predicts Soft-Closure Error}\label{sec:reconvergence-exp}
On elementary structures, a $9\times9$ grid of priors confirms Theorem~\ref{thm:star}: the soft closure equals exact WMC to at most $5\times10^{-13}$ on the single edge, the three-child star, and the two-parent polytree. On the diamond, the actual loopy-BP implementation differs by up to $5.8\times10^{-2}$ over the grid, while the Lean path-independent soft-OR witness gives $5/6$ versus $9/10$ at uniform priors. Both checks validate the same failure mode: the approximation is not globally arbitrary; it appears when two derivational paths reuse evidence.

On GO cc, where $\tw=12$ permits exact calibration of the full namespace, the same pattern appears at ontology scale. The soft closure has mean absolute error $2.1\times10^{-4}$ against exact junction-tree WMC, with maximum error $6.4\times10^{-2}$ and only $0.6\%$ of terms above $10^{-2}$. Error is $6.0\times$ larger at multi-parent terms than at single-parent terms ($7.4\times10^{-4}$ vs.\ $1.2\times10^{-4}$), and it grows with in-degree (Fig.~\ref{fig:indegree}). A purely upward soft-OR, which omits the downward true-path cap, has mean error $3.4\times10^{-2}$; the effective closure is therefore bidirectional BP, not a one-way heuristic.

\subsubsection{Core Selection and Separator Rank}\label{sec:design-exp}
The modular result depends on two choices: which terms enter the reconvergence core, and how much dependence the boundary separator can represent. The residual-width probe in Table~\ref{tab:ontology-struct} shows that high-degree cuts are a useful diagnostic, but Table~\ref{tab:modular} uses a stricter boundary-aware objective: every peripheral component must remain low-treewidth after its adjacent core boundary $B$ is added back. This is why the paper reports both residual treewidth and augmented-module treewidth.

The separator rank controls the remaining approximation on the core. Over the 40 highest-degree GO cc reconvergence cores, the exact boundary joint is consistently non-product: median $\mathrm{KL}(q^\star\Vert\text{product})=1.49$ nats, with exact tensor-train rank median $5$ (range $3$--$10$). On a representative 10-term core, rank~1 has TV $0.68$, rank~2 closes $46\%$ of the gap, rank~4 closes $82\%$, and rank~8 is exact to numerical precision (Table~\ref{tab:rank}). Thus the soft closure is the rank-1 endpoint of a continuous accuracy-cost dial, rather than a separate algorithm.

\subsection{Downstream Protein Function Prediction over GO}\label{sec:downstream}
\paragraph{Task and comparison principle.}
The downstream experiment evaluates whether WMC-based ontology post-processing is useful on standard protein-function prediction over GO \citep{radivojac2013cafa,zhou2019cafa3}. We do not compare protein encoders. Instead, we use fixed published predictors and apply different ontology post-processing layers to the same predicted term scores $p_i$. Thus any difference in Tables~\ref{tab:downstream-within}--\ref{tab:downstream-boundary} is attributable to the post-processing layer, not to a stronger backbone.

\paragraph{Within-vocabulary prediction is neutral.}
For terms already present in the predictor vocabulary, the training labels are already propagated through the GO hierarchy. Re-imposing the hierarchy at inference therefore has little room to help. Table~\ref{tab:downstream-within} shows representative fixed-MLP runs: WMC changes Fmax by at most $0.007$ relative to raw scores, while hard max-propagation can hurt by over-inflating ancestors. On bp, for example, max-propagation drops Fmax from $0.458$ to $0.420$, while normalized WMC remains close to raw.

\paragraph{Downstream interpretation.}
The downstream result is therefore not a blanket improvement over protein-function predictors. It is a competence-boundary result: WMC helps when ontology definitions add external compositional structure and the base retains signal on the components; it is neutral when the term is already supervised or when the base has collapsed on all components.

\subsection{Limits}\label{sec:limits}
The remaining limits are threefold. Rank-1 modular inference is exact on the periphery but approximate on the reconvergence core; the rank-$r$ separator removes this approximation at additional cost. NF2 conjunctions increase augmented-module width and therefore move some terms from the exact periphery into the core. Full SROIQ disjunction creates multimodal boundary joints, where independence assumptions fail qualitatively; Appendix~\ref{app:nesy} reports this controlled disjunctive regime separately.


\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}
We study structure-aware posterior inference for independent predictor scores constrained by large hierarchical ontologies. The common soft closure is loopy belief propagation on the true-path constraint graph: exact on tree-like structure and wrong only at reconvergences. Separator-aware modular exact WMC recovers exact marginals on most concepts by isolating a small reconvergence core; a rank-$r$ separator interpolates between the fast independence closure and exact inference. On Gene Ontology, cutting 0--9\% cores yields 91--100\% modular-exact coverage across the cc, mf, and bp namespaces; the same pipeline reaches 95\% modular-exact coverage on UBERON and 98\% on ChEBI. The layer maps independent predictor scores to structure-aware marginals without retraining, with NeSy concept layers as one application rather than the only setting. Remaining limits include full SROIQ disjunction, rank-1 error on reconvergent cores, and learned approximators that require per-ontology training.

\section{Application Note}
The inference layer is a drop-in posterior. \section{Ontology Instantiation Details}\label{app:multi-ontology}

\paragraph{Reproducibility identifiers.}
The ontology snapshots are GO \citep{geneontology2023} \texttt{go-basic.obo} 2025-06, UBERON \citep{mungall2012uberon} \texttt{uberon-basic.owl} 2026-06-19, and ChEBI \citep{hastings2016chebi} release 252 (2026-05-01). We instantiate each ontology by retaining live terms, mapping subsumption edges to implications $y_c\Rightarrow y_\pi$, and adding EL\textsuperscript{++} normal-form conjunctions when the normalized theory is used. GO is evaluated by namespace; UBERON and ChEBI are evaluated as single-ontology transfer cases.

\paragraph{Structure and modular inference protocol.}
For each ontology row in Tables~\ref{tab:ontology-struct} and~\ref{tab:modular}, we compute term count, edge count, multi-parent fraction, maximum in-degree, monolithic treewidth, residual treewidth after high-degree reconvergence cuts, boundary-aware core size, number of peripheral modules, maximum augmented-module treewidth, exact-coverage rate, and wall time. The residual-width probe removes high-degree terms only to diagnose where treewidth concentrates. The modular pipeline then reselects a boundary-aware core so that each exact component is solved as $M\cup B$, where $B$ is the adjacent core boundary.

\paragraph{Validation protocol.}
On every module small enough for enumeration, the junction-tree WMC marginal is checked against brute force. On monolithic GO cc, where $\tw=12$, the full soft closure is compared directly against exact junction-tree WMC, giving the error localization results in Fig.~\ref{fig:indegree}. On larger ontologies, exact comparison is performed on sampled low-treewidth cones and on the peripheral modules certified by the modular construction.

\paragraph{Baseline protocol.}
DeepProbLog-style exact compilation is evaluated on increasing GO bp subgraphs until compilation walls. Scallop is evaluated with the same true-path provenance query on 200 GO bp neighborhoods and top-$k$ values $k\in\{1,2,3,5,10,20,50\}$. A-NeSI and NeSyDM are kept in Appendix~\ref{app:nesy} as learned-inference comparisons on carry chains, because they require task-specific training and are not drop-in ontology posterior layers.

\section{Formalisation: implementation and lemma inventory}\label{app:lean}

\paragraph{A kernel-checked development with decidable rational arithmetic.}
The Lean~4 development (\texttt{Wmc.lean}, Lean 4.31) is a single Lean-core file with no
dependence on \texttt{mathlib}. It defines worlds as Boolean lists, the true-path constraint
as a conjunction of edge clauses $(\neg y_c \vee y_\pi)$, the exact weighted model count as a
ratio of summed world weights (\texttt{marginal}), and the soft-OR update (\texttt{softOR}),
and it closes every claim by \texttt{decide}, reduction in the kernel, with no
\texttt{native\_decide} escape hatch and therefore no trust placed in compiled code. To keep
rational arithmetic cheap in the kernel we use a \emph{custom rational encoding}. A rational
is a pair $Q=\mathbb{Z}\times\mathbb{Z}$ of numerator and denominator, with addition,
multiplication and complementation ($1-a$) defined directly on the pair, and equality the
\emph{cross-multiplied} test $a_1 b_2 = b_1 a_2$, decidable on integers without normalizing the
fraction. Hence world enumeration, weighting, summation, and the soft-OR/WMC comparison are all
reducible by \texttt{decide}. \texttt{\#print axioms} reports every theorem depends on
$\{\texttt{propext}\}$ alone, with no \texttt{Classical.choice}, no \texttt{Quot.sound}, no
\texttt{sorry}.

\paragraph{Parametric development and lemma inventory.}
The general Theorem~\ref{thm:star}, over all $k:\mathbb{N}$ and all real priors, is proved in a
separate \texttt{mathlib} development (\texttt{WmcStar.star\_marginal\_eq\_softOR}, Lean 4.31).
It models worlds as \texttt{Bool}\,$\times$\,(\texttt{Fin}\,$k\to$\texttt{Bool}), uses the
product-of-sums interchange \texttt{Finset.prod\_univ\_sum} to evaluate the parent-true mass to
$q_\pi$ and \texttt{Finset.sum\_eq\_single} to collapse the parent-false case to the all-false
world, giving $Z=q_\pi+(1-q_\pi)\prod_i(1-q_i)$ and the soft-OR ratio. Its axiom footprint is
$\{\texttt{propext},\texttt{Classical.choice},\texttt{Quot.sound}\}$ with no \texttt{sorry}. The
concrete Lean-core development additionally checks the instances directly. The single edge
(\texttt{single\_edge\_exact}) and the $k{=}2,3$ stars (\texttt{star2\_exact},
\texttt{star3\_exact}) reduce both sides to the same $Q$ by \texttt{decide}. The reconvergence is the diamond
($d\!\Rightarrow\!b,c$, $b,c\!\Rightarrow\!a$). \texttt{diamond\_exact\_5\_6} proves the exact
reconvergent marginal is $5/6$. \texttt{diamond\_softor\_9\_10} proves the soft-OR update (the
two paths merged independently) is $9/10$. \texttt{diamond\_softor\_ne\_exact} proves they
are unequal, so the soft closure is exact on the tree fragment and provably inexact at the
reconvergence. Unconditional convergence of the monotone closure is the finite-vocabulary
least-fixpoint argument of \S\ref{sec:lean} (the synchronous step is extensive and monotone, so
it stabilizes within the graph depth). We verify the exact/soft values rather than re-prove the
fixpoint in Lean.

\section{Extended neuro-symbolic comparison numbers}\label{app:nesy}

\paragraph{Top-$k$ provenance (Scallop).}
The four-node reconvergent derivation has two proof paths to a shared fact via the upward rule $\texttt{holds}(P)\!:\!-\,\texttt{sub}(C,P),\texttt{holds}(C)$ at edge-probability $\tfrac12$, a construction distinct from the Lean true-path diamond of \S\ref{sec:lean} (different inference semantics, so its exact value $0.9375$ does not conflict with $5/6$ on that diamond). Run in the actual Scallop system (\texttt{scallopy}, built from source, top-$k$-proofs), the exact marginal $P(\texttt{holds}(a))=0.9375$ is returned as $0.50$ at $k{=}1$ (a $47\%$ undercount), $0.875$ at $k{=}3$, and the exact $0.9375$ at $k{=}10$, identical to our exact-semantics reference. On real biological-process neighbourhoods the maximum drift versus the exact provenance reference falls from $0.34$ at $k{=}1$ (over 200 targets, $0.21$ on a $14$-term cone run in \texttt{scallopy}) to below $10^{-3}$ at $k{=}10$. The error therefore grows with proof multiplicity (reconvergence) and shrinks as $k$ admits more proofs, the same reconvergence axis along which our soft closure departs from exact $\WMC$.

\paragraph{The carry-chain: exact independence vs.\ its sampled estimate.}
We instantiate the $N$-digit carry chain as MNIST addition under distant (sum-only) supervision,
training a shared digit CNN with the independence-assumption marginal of the sum and reporting
test sum-accuracy over three seeds. With the \emph{exact} independence product (computed by
convolving the per-position digit distributions) the model degrades only gently as the carry
couples more digits: $0.970/0.942/0.926/0.905$ for $N{=}1$ to $4$, the per-digit error
compounding rather than a collapse. Its bare \emph{Monte-Carlo} estimate, the sampling a naive
learned objective rests on, instead collapses over the same range, $0.129/0.010/0.001/0.000$,
as the probability that a sample satisfies the constraint vanishes and the gradient disappears.
This is the independence-assumption / reasoning-shortcut failure
\citep{vankrieken2024independence,marconato2023reasoningshortcuts} that learned inference engines
are built to escape, and they do. Run in the \emph{actual} A-NeSI system on the same benchmark
($20$ epochs/seed), sum-accuracy is $0.973/0.922/0.875/0.748$ from $N{=}1$ to $4$, a graceful
degradation tracking the exact product, not the collapse of the sampler, because A-NeSI learns the
inference distribution rather than sampling the constraint. The cost is moved, not removed. Neither
A-NeSI nor NeSyDM \citep{vankrieken2025nesydm} can be run on GO (each needs a model trained per
ontology, and A-NeSI needs samples of the exact $\WMC$ we compute), which is exactly what the
training-free exact construction of this paper supplies.

\paragraph{The disjunctive (SROIQ) regime: a multimodal joint.}
The monotone true-path theory gives a unimodal, merely correlated boundary joint. Disjunction is the
qualitative break. On a controlled fragment over $w{=}6$ boundary atoms, two entities, each typed by

disjointness, the exact joint is \emph{multimodal} ($6$ modes), and the IA cannot represent it.

rank-$r$ dial is exact only at full rank $3$ of the joint (Table~\ref{tab:disj}). This is the
regime that the disjoint-cube result of van Krieken rules out for the IA, and the one a SROIQ extension targets.
