\begin{abstract}
\noindent Neuro-symbolic learning couples a neural perception model with symbolic reasoning under a
\emph{conditional-independence assumption}: the perception model emits independent per-concept
marginals and the symbolic layer treats them as an independent product. Repairing this assumption
exactly requires weighted model counting (WMC) over the symbolic theory, which is $\#$P-hard and, for
a real ontology with tens of thousands of axioms, far beyond exact reasoning. We study the soft,
message-passing closure used in practice and give it the two properties a deployable inference layer
needs: a \emph{correctness characterization} and \emph{tractability at ontology scale}. First, we
prove in machine-checked Lean~4 that the soft closure of the true-path theory is loopy belief
propagation, and is the \emph{exact} WMC marginal on the tree fragment for \emph{all} priors, with
error confined to reconvergent (multi-parent) structures (no \texttt{sorry}; core axioms
$\subseteq\{\texttt{propext},\texttt{Quot.sound}\}$, the general theorem in mathlib). Second, we show
the Gene Ontology constraint graph has treewidth $9$/$31$/$272$ over its three namespaces, so exact
monolithic WMC is infeasible on the largest, but removing only a ${<}8\%$ reconvergence core makes
$92$--$100\%$ of the ontology exactly computable by a \emph{modular} construction---each
low-treewidth module compiled to a sentential decision diagram with weighted separator messages, the
bounded soft approximation confined to a small core. Third, we run the canonical neuro-symbolic
frameworks on the identical ontology inference: exact knowledge compilation (DeepProbLog) walls at
${\sim}400$ terms; top-$k$ provenance (Scallop) scales but its drift concentrates exactly at the
reconvergences our theory identifies; and learned approximators (A-NeSI, NeSyDM) collapse on coupled
structure. The result is an inference layer that is simultaneously scalable and accuracy-characterized,
demonstrated on protein function prediction in a companion paper.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Neuro-symbolic (NeSy) learning couples a neural perception model with symbolic reasoning, almost
universally under a \emph{conditional-independence assumption}: the perception model emits
independent per-concept marginals, and the symbolic layer treats them as an independent product
\citep{manhaeve2018deepproblog,xu2018semanticloss,ahmed2022spl}. The assumption is known to impede
optimization, distort uncertainty, and admit \emph{reasoning shortcuts}
\citep{vankrieken2024independence,marconato2023reasoningshortcuts,marconato2024bears}. Repairing it
means conditioning the factorized perception distribution on the symbolic theory, whose marginals are
ratios of weighted model counts (WMC). Exact WMC is $\#$P-hard \citep{chavira2008wmc}; on a
description-logic ontology with tens of thousands of axioms it is far beyond exact probabilistic
reasoning, and this is precisely where NeSy systems fall back on soft, message-passing relaxations
whose correctness is rarely characterized.

We close that gap for the \emph{true-path theory}---the propositional grounding of an ontology's
hierarchical (is-a/part-of) and definitional axioms that arises, e.g., in protein function prediction
over the Gene Ontology (GO). Our contributions are:
\begin{enumerate}\itemsep1pt
\item \textbf{Machine-checked exactness.} We formalize the soft closure in Lean~4
\citep{demoura2021lean,mathlib2020} and prove it is belief propagation, exact on the tree fragment for
\emph{all} priors, with error confined to reconvergences (\S\ref{sec:lean}).
\item \textbf{Modular exact WMC at ontology scale.} We measure the GO constraint-graph treewidth and
show that cutting a ${<}8\%$ reconvergence core makes $92$--$100\%$ of GO exactly computable by a
modular SDD construction (\S\ref{sec:scaling}).
\item \textbf{A full NeSy-framework comparison} on the identical inference, placing exact compilation,
top-$k$ provenance, and learned approximators against the characterized regime our theory predicts
(\S\ref{sec:nesy}).
\end{enumerate}

\section{The symbolic theory and its soft closure}\label{sec:setup}
\paragraph{Independent perception.}
Let $\mathcal{Y}=\{1,\dots,m\}$ be a target vocabulary and $f_\theta$ a perception model emitting
independent marginals $p_i\in[0,1]$, inducing $q(\bC y)=\prod_i p_i^{y_i}(1-p_i)^{1-y_i}$ over
$\bC y\in\{0,1\}^m$.

\begin{definition}[True-path theory]
A theory $T$ is a propositional formula over $\mathcal Y$. The \emph{true-path} theory is
$T=\bigwedge_{(c,\pi)\in E}(y_c\!\Rightarrow\!y_\pi)\wedge\bigwedge_{A\sqcap B\sqsubseteq E}(y_A\wedge
y_B\!\Rightarrow\!y_E)\wedge(\text{existential defs})$: the hierarchy edges $E$ plus the
EL\textsuperscript{++} normal-form definitions \citep{baader2005elenvelope,kazakov2014elk}.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[WMC marginal]
The structure-conditioned posterior is $p_T(\bC y)\propto[\bC y\models T]\,q(\bC y)$, with target
marginal
\[
\mu_i=p_T(y_i{=}1)=\frac{\WMC(T\wedge y_i;\bC p)}{\WMC(T;\bC p)},\qquad
\WMC(\phi;\bC p)=\!\!\sum_{\bC y\models\phi}\prod_i p_i^{y_i}(1-p_i)^{1-y_i}.
\]
\end{definition}
This is the semantic-probabilistic-layer construction \citep{ahmed2022spl,xu2018semanticloss};
exact evaluation proceeds by knowledge compilation to a sentential decision diagram (SDD) or d-DNNF
\citep{darwiche2002kcmap,darwiche2011sdd}, whose cost is governed by treewidth (\S\ref{sec:scaling}).

\paragraph{Soft closure.}
In place of exact compilation, the deployed inference is the monotone message-passing fixpoint of the
EL completion \citep{kazakov2014elk}: each node aggregates a normalized soft-OR of its sufficient
conditions and is capped from above by the true-path constraint. This is loopy belief propagation
\citep{pearl1988probabilistic} on the constraint graph. The remainder of the paper characterizes when
it is exact, makes it tractable at scale, and compares it to the NeSy spectrum.

\section{The soft closure is belief propagation: machine-checked exactness}\label{sec:lean}
We formalize the soft closure in Lean~4 \citep{demoura2021lean}. The core development is in Lean core
(no \texttt{sorry}; axioms $\subseteq\{\texttt{propext},\texttt{Quot.sound}\}$); the general theorem
uses mathlib \citep{mathlib2020}.

\begin{theorem}[Tree-exactness, all priors]\label{thm:star}
For a target $\pi$ with $k$ independent children $c_1,\dots,c_k$ under the true-path constraint
$y_{c_j}\!\Rightarrow\!y_\pi$, the exact WMC marginal of $\pi$ equals the soft-OR update
\[
\mu_\pi=\frac{q_\pi}{q_\pi+(1-q_\pi)\prod_{j=1}^{k}(1-q_{c_j})}
\]
for all $k$ and all priors $q_\pi,q_{c_j}$ (Lean: \texttt{star\_marginal\_eq\_softOR}).
\end{theorem}

Belief propagation is exact on trees \citep{pearl1988probabilistic}; Theorem~\ref{thm:star} is the
machine-checked instance for the true-path theory. The error is localized at \emph{reconvergences}:
on a diamond (two paths to a shared ancestor) the exact marginal is $5/6$ while the soft-OR gives
$7/10$ (Lean: \texttt{diamond\_reconv\_inexact}), and a finite breadth battery
(\texttt{WMCBattery.lean}) confirms by kernel computation that single-edge BP equals exact WMC and
the polytree is exact across an $81$-prior grid, while the diamond is inexact. Convergence of the
closure is unconditional (\texttt{step\_mono}, \texttt{fixed\_stable}); only its limit is
structure-dependent. The custom rational kernel encoding (cross-multiplied $\mathbb{Z}\times\mathbb{Z}$
equality, reducible by \texttt{decide}) is what makes the breadth battery checkable in the kernel.

\section{Tractability: treewidth and modular exact WMC}\label{sec:scaling}
Exact WMC by compilation costs $\Theta(m\,2^{\tw})$ in the constraint-graph treewidth $\tw$
\citep{darwiche2011sdd,bodlaender1993treewidth}. We measure the GO constraint graph:
$\tw=9$ (cellular component), $31$ (molecular function), $272$ (biological process). On cellular
component, exact junction-tree WMC runs in $7.5$\,s and the soft closure agrees to mean error
$6\times10^{-4}$, $7\times$ larger at multi-parent nodes---precisely Theorem~\ref{thm:star}'s
reconvergence error, empirically localized.

Treewidth is a graph invariant, so the biological-process namespace ($\tw=272$, a $2^{272}$ circuit)
admits \emph{no} exact monolithic WMC. But the high treewidth is carried by a small reconvergent core:
cutting only the highest-degree reconvergence hubs makes the remainder exactly computable. Removing
$2.95\%$ of molecular-function terms leaves $97\%$ exact; removing $7.7\%$ of biological-process terms
leaves $92\%$ exact. Hence $92$--$100\%$ of GO admits exact \emph{modular} WMC:

\begin{proposition}[Modular exact WMC]\label{prop:modular}
Partition the constraint graph into low-treewidth modules separated by the reconvergence core. Compile
each module to an SDD, evaluate its exact WMC, and pass weighted separator marginals between modules;
the result is exact on the union of modules, with the bounded soft approximation
(Theorem~\ref{thm:star}) confined to the small core.
\end{proposition}

A principled module decomposition can be supplied by locality-based modules
\citep{cuencagrau2008modular,delvescovo2011atomic} or by the consequence-based context calculus
\citep{bate2016sriq,tenacucala2019sequoia}; lifting the latter from Boolean consequences to weighted
separator messages is the route to a verified modular WMC over the whole ontology.

\section{Comparison to neuro-symbolic frameworks}\label{sec:nesy}
We run the canonical NeSy tools on the identical GO true-path inference (Table~\ref{tab:nesy}). Both
the ProbLog engine of DeepProbLog and Scallop reproduce our exact marginals on validation cases (the
diamond $5/6$; the chain $0.875$), so the comparison is apples-to-apples.

\paragraph{Exact compilation walls.}
The ProbLog engine reproduces our exact marginals but, via knowledge compilation, times out
($>300$\,s) at ${\sim}400$ terms---one to two orders of magnitude below GO's $2.8$k (cc), $6.9$k (mf),
$21$k (bp) terms. The exact-NeSy wall is empirical here and rigorous via the treewidth probe.

\paragraph{Top-$k$ provenance scales but drifts exactly at reconvergences.}
Scallop's \texttt{topkproofs} runs full biological process ($21{,}324$ terms in $0.9$\,s), but
top-$k$ truncates proofs and the error lives exactly at reconvergent (multi-proof) nodes---the regime
Theorem~\ref{thm:star} characterizes. For the diamond, $P(\text{holds}(a))$ exact $0.9375$ is
approximated as $0.50$ ($k{=}1$), $0.875$ ($k{=}3$), $0.9375$ ($k{=}10$); on $14$-node balls the max
drift falls from $0.15$--$0.30$ ($k{=}1$) to $0.000$ ($k{=}10$). At full-GO scale (bp circuit rank
$24{,}432$) a hub term has astronomically many proofs, so any fixed $k$ systematically undercounts and
the error is \emph{unverifiable}, no exact reference being computable---the $\#$P-hardness made
concrete. Scallop scales by giving up the guarantee.

\paragraph{Learned approximators relocate the wall to coupled structure.}
A-NeSI \citep{vankrieken2023anesi} (amortized inference) and NeSyDM \citep{vankrieken2025nesydm}
(masked discrete diffusion over the symbolic variables) are end-to-end training methods, not inference
engines; A-NeSI's surrogate must moreover be trained on samples of the exact WMC, intractable to
generate at GO scale. On the carry-chain benchmark, where the constraint couples $N$ digits, both
collapse as depth grows: A-NeSI $0.97\!\to\!0.23$ and NeSyDM $0.96\!\to\!0.03$ from $N{=}1$ to $N{=}4$.
The learned-approximate route does not escape the wall---it relocates it from exact-inference cost to
approximation quality on coupled structure, again without a guarantee.

\paragraph{Positioning.}
Across the spectrum---exact compilation, top-$k$ provenance, amortized inference, joint diffusion---each
trades correctness for scale and offers no guarantee on the coupled-structure regime. Ours is the only
option that is simultaneously scalable and accuracy-characterized: machine-checked exact on the tree
fragment, modular-exact over $92$--$100\%$ of GO, and bounded-approximate only on the small reconvergent
core, rather than top-$k$-truncated everywhere.

\section{Discussion}
The soft closure that NeSy systems use under the independence assumption is not a black box: for the
true-path theory it is belief propagation, machine-checked exact on the tree fragment for all priors,
with its single failure mode---reconvergence---both identified by the theory and confirmed to carry
the empirical error (the cc agreement to $6\times10^{-4}$, Scallop's drift, the treewidth core). The
practical consequence is a modular construction that recovers exact WMC over almost all of a real
ontology while bounding the approximation to a small, characterized core. Two extensions follow:
lifting the consequence-based context calculus \citep{bate2016sriq,tenacucala2019sequoia} from Boolean
consequences to weighted separator messages for a fully verified modular WMC, and extending the
EL\textsuperscript{++} compilation toward SROIQ \citep{horrocks2006sroiq,motik2009hypertableau}. An
application of this inference layer to protein function prediction---where it gives any base predictor
training-free, similarity-independent reach over ontology structure---is developed in a companion
paper.
